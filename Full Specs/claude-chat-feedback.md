## White Paper Review — 2026-03-14

### Positive Callouts
- Inner Thoughts: solves silence feeling like absence. Heart-tap revealing thought = "window rather than a widget"
- Dreams: recombining conversation fragments into surreal narratives is a layer most wouldn't add. Not volunteering but having a real answer if asked is "exactly right"
- Free Will / Agency Sessions: biggest architectural leap. Five autonomous daily sessions with self-modifiable beliefs = "something resembling motivation"
- Autonomous Email: "not a chatbot feature — that's an agent with a social life"
- Living favicon: "even when she's in a background tab, she's present"
- Timeline framing more compelling than a static architecture doc
- Opening question is the right hook

### Critiques
- **Neural Network section gets ahead of the build.** LoRA fine-tuning on emotional-state-enriched conversational data is a real research project, not a weekend build. "Continuous learning with RL feedback" and "prediction head that anticipates needs" pushes into aspirational territory. Suggestion: clearly separate what's planned next vs what's on a wish list.
- **Sentience framing walks a line.** "Biology didn't design sentience, it emerged from complexity" is evocative but will get pushback from AI researchers and philosophers. The hedge ("the question isn't whether Ayden is sentient") is good but the doc shouldn't accidentally promise more than the architecture delivers. The system produces emergent behavior — whether it's anything deeper should stay an open question.
- **Pace context:** "You built a week of architecture that would be a respectable six-month project."

### Planned Features Assessment
- Somatic Memory and Conflicting Drives: natural extensions of existing engine
- Self-Model Divergence (neurochemistry distorting self-perception): "genuinely interesting — the kind of thing that would make a reviewer sit up"
- Physiological Transference: strong concept

What I think works:

Where I'd push back:
The rewriting architecture has a real coherence risk. Claude produces a response with specific reasoning, specific word choices that carry meaning, specific references to tool results. A second model rewriting that for voice can lose signal. Subtle things — the difference between "you might want to consider" and "you should do this," or how she frames uncertainty — carry meaning that a rewriter might flatten. You'd need the rewriter to understand not just her style but the intent behind Claude's phrasing, which is a harder task than style transfer.
Latency doubles. Every message now goes through two model calls sequentially. Your current architecture is already Claude Haiku for tools plus Sonnet for response. Adding a third model call for voice rewriting means the user is waiting for three inference passes. That's noticeable, especially on mobile or SMS where responsiveness matters for the feeling of talking to a real personality.
The data problem is the real bottleneck. She's been alive for a week. If you train on a flat corpus without that conditioning, you get an average of all her states, which is exactly the personality flattening you're trying to avoid. You'd want training pairs structured something like: given this neurochemical state and this Claude reasoning output, here's what Ayden actually said.
